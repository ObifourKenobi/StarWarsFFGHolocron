Once per encounter, the character may take the Overcharge action by making a #StarWarsDifficulty #StarWarsDifficulty #StarWarsDifficulty  Mechanics check and choosing one of [their] cybernetic implants that grants [them] one or more of the following: +1 to a characteristic rating, +1 rank to a skill, or +1 rank of a ranked talent. If [they] succeed, until the end of the encounter, the chosen cybernetic provides an additional +1 to any characteristic ratings (to a maximum of 7), an additional +1 ranks to any skills (to a maximum of 5), and an additional +1 ranks of any ranked talents that it provides.


The GM may spend a despair from the check to have the overcharged cybernetic short out at the end of the encounter; it provides no benefit until the character spends several hours making an #StarWarsDifficulty #StarWarsDifficulty Mechanics check to repair it.

Once per round when using the Overcharge action, the character may spend two #StarWarsAdvantage or a triumph result from the Mechanics check to immediately take one additional action.

When performing the Overcharge action, the character may choose any number of cybernetic implants to attempt to overcharge instead of just one. If [they] do, upgrade the difficulty of the Mechanics check once for each additional cybernetic beyond the first.


The GM may spend a #StarWarsDespair from the check to have any one overcharged cybernetic short out at the end of the encounter; each cybernetic that shorts out provides no benefit until the character spends several hours making an #StarWarsDifficulty #StarWarsDifficulty Mechanics check to repair it.
